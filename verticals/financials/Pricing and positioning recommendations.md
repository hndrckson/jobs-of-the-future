# PQC Pricing and Positioning Recommendations for Financial Services

## Strategic Intelligence Brief — March 2026

---

## Executive Summary

PQC for financial services is still a nascent market, which means pricing is not yet anchored by established benchmarks or competitive pressure. This is both an opportunity (premium pricing is possible) and a challenge (buyers have no reference points, which creates friction). The pricing strategy must balance three tensions: (1) high enough to signal enterprise credibility and fund sustained development, (2) low enough to win initial reference accounts, (3) structured to create recurring revenue and expansion dynamics.

**Core pricing insight**: Banks don't buy PQC for PQC's sake. They buy regulatory risk reduction, examination preparedness, HNDL exposure mitigation, and demonstrable due diligence. Price against the *risk avoided*, not the *technology delivered*.

### The Key Insight: Banks Spend More Than Other Industries

Financial services pays a **15–30% premium** over other industries for the same security capabilities due to:
- Regulatory compliance requirements (FFIEC, OCC, SEC, FINRA)
- Higher security maturity expectations
- Stricter vendor management requirements (SOC 2, ISO 27001, penetration testing)
- More complex integration environments
- Greater liability and reputational risk

## Recommended Pricing Architecture

### The Three-Phase Model

PQC migration is not a point-in-time purchase — it's a multi-year journey. The pricing architecture should mirror the migration lifecycle and create natural expansion dynamics.

### Phase 1: Discovery and Assessment ($150K–$750K)

**What's included**:
- Cryptographic inventory and discovery across enterprise
- HNDL risk quantification by data type and business unit
- Regulatory gap analysis (FFIEC, OCC, Fed, SEC mapping)
- Board-ready quantum risk assessment report
- PQC migration roadmap with prioritized remediation plan
- Examination preparation documentation

**Pricing model**: Fixed-fee engagement with defined deliverables

**Pricing by bank size**:

| Bank Tier | Assets | Price Range | Rationale |
|-----------|--------|-------------|-----------|
| G-SIB / Tier 1 | >$250B | $500K–$750K | Complex multi-geography crypto estate; board-level deliverables; regulatory scrutiny |
| Large regional | $50–250B | $300K–$500K | Significant but less complex estate; regulatory expectations ramping |
| Mid-size | $10–50B | $150K–$300K | Focused assessment; preparing for upcoming exam cycle |

**Strategic pricing considerations**:
- **Undercut Big 4 advisory**: EY/Deloitte would charge $750K–$2M for equivalent advisory engagements. Position at 40–60% of Big 4 pricing with better technology-enabled deliverables
- **Loss-leader acceptable for first 3–5 reference accounts**: Consider pricing at $100K–$200K for initial bank references to build the portfolio
- **Always include a board-ready deliverable**: This ensures the engagement reaches the decision-makers who approve Phase 2 spending

### Phase 2: Platform License and Migration ($500K–$5M ACV)

**What's included**:
- PQC platform license (discovery, monitoring, orchestration)
- Crypto agility management
- Automated compliance monitoring and reporting
- Integration with existing HSMs (Thales Luna, Entrust nShield)
- Migration orchestration tools
- Ongoing crypto posture dashboard

**Pricing models** (choose based on customer preference):

#### Model A: Enterprise License (Preferred for Large Banks)

| Component | Annual Price | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Base platform license | $250K–$1M | Per-institution; covers unlimited users |
| Crypto asset monitoring | $100K–$500K | Based on number of crypto assets discovered |
| Migration orchestration | $200K–$1M | Based on number of systems in migration |
| Regulatory reporting module | $50K–$150K | Automated FFIEC/OCC/SEC mapping |
| Premium support | $100K–$300K | Dedicated support engineer; 4-hour SLA |
| **Total ACV range** | **$700K–$3M** | |

#### Model B: Per-Crypto-Asset Pricing (Preferred for Mid-Size Banks)

| Component | Price | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Discovery platform | $100K–$200K base | Platform access |
| Per crypto asset discovered | $50–$200/asset/year | Scales with estate size; typical bank has 10K–100K+ crypto assets |
| Migration orchestration per system | $5K–$25K/system | One-time migration fee per application/system |
| Ongoing monitoring per asset | $25–$100/asset/year | Post-migration continuous monitoring |

**Why per-asset works**: A mid-size bank with 25,000 crypto assets at $100/asset = $2.5M ACV. This creates clear unit economics and natural expansion as more assets are discovered.

#### Model C: Managed Service (For Banks Without In-House Crypto Expertise)

| Component | Monthly Price | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Managed crypto inventory and monitoring | $25K–$75K/month | Fully managed by vendor team |
| Managed migration execution | $50K–$150K/month | Active migration management |
| Compliance reporting and exam preparation | $10K–$25K/month | Continuous regulatory posture management |
| **Total annual range** | **$1M–$3M** | |

**Why managed services work for banking**: Many mid-size banks lack in-house cryptographic expertise. A managed service removes the talent bottleneck and provides predictable cost. Managed services also create the stickiest customer relationships.

### Phase 3: Ongoing Operations ($300K–$2M ACV)

**What's included** (post-migration):
- Continuous crypto posture monitoring
- Algorithmic agility management (future algorithm updates)
- Regulatory change management
- Annual assessment refresh
- Incident response support for crypto-related issues

**Pricing model**: Annual subscription, 20–30% of Phase 2 platform price

## Deal Structure Recommendations

### Multi-Year Contracts (Recommended)

| Term | Discount from Annual | Rationale |
|------|---------------------|-----------|
| 1 year | List price | Initial engagement; builds trust |
| 3 years | 10–15% discount | Standard bank procurement preference; predictable revenue |
| 5 years | 15–25% discount | Strategic partnership; long-term lock-in; aligns with PQC migration timeline |

**Banks strongly prefer multi-year agreements** for several reasons:
- Budget predictability across fiscal years
- Vendor stability assurance (if you're committed for 5 years, you're more likely to survive)
- Regulatory continuity (consistent tool for examinations across multiple cycles)
- Procurement efficiency (one approval process instead of annual renewal)

### Land-and-Expand Dynamics

```
Year 1: Discovery + Assessment ($300K)
    → Proves value, identifies crypto estate, gets board attention
Year 2: Platform license for high-priority systems ($750K)
    → First PQC migrations in M&A, trading communications
Year 3: Enterprise expansion ($1.5M)
    → All critical systems under management; compliance automation
Year 4: Full estate coverage ($2M+)
    → Complete crypto posture management
Year 5+: Ongoing operations ($1.5M renewal)
    → Steady-state monitoring and agility management
```

**5-year total contract value**: $6M+ from a single large bank

### The "Reference Account" Discount

For the first 3–5 bank customers, offer a structured discount in exchange for:
- Named reference (public or confidential)
- Case study participation
- Joint speaking at industry events (FS-ISAC, BITS)
- Willingness to take calls from prospective customers

**Suggested reference discount**: 30–50% off Year 1, 15–25% off Years 2–3, standard pricing Year 4+

**Why this works**: A bank CISO will say "I got a great deal because I was an early adopter and helped shape the product." This is a story that resonates with other CISOs — they all want to be the smart early mover, not the one who paid list price later.

## Pricing Psychology for Banking Buyers

### Price Anchoring

Anchor against the *cost of the problem*, not the cost of alternative solutions:

| Anchor Point | Value |
|-------------|-------|
| Average data breach cost in financial services | $5.9M (IBM Cost of a Data Breach 2024) |
| HNDL exposure for a G-SIB (estimated) | $800M–$8B (see companion HNDL analysis) |
| SEC enforcement action for cybersecurity failure | $200M+ (JPMorgan off-channel communications) |
| FINRA enforcement actions (annual) | ~$5B total across 784 actions (2023) |
| Regulatory MRA remediation cost | $2M–$20M per finding |
| Reputational damage from crypto breach | Incalculable |

**Framing**: "Your HNDL exposure on M&A communications alone exceeds $500M. Our Phase 1 assessment costs $500K — that's 0.1% of the risk we're helping you quantify and mitigate."

### The "Insurance" Frame

Position PQC not as a technology purchase but as **risk insurance**:
- "What would you pay to guarantee that your encrypted M&A communications remain confidential through 2035?"
- "If a quantum computer breaks RSA tomorrow, what's the value of having already migrated your most sensitive data?"

### Avoiding Price Sensitivity Traps

- **Don't price per-seat** — banks have tens of thousands of employees; per-seat pricing creates sticker shock and procurement friction
- **Don't price per-transaction** — payment/trading volume makes this unpredictable and creates budget anxiety
- **Do price per-asset or per-system** — creates clear unit economics that procurement can model
- **Do include compliance value** — always bundle regulatory reporting and examination preparation; this justifies premium pricing

## Positioning Recommendations

### Positioning Statement (Financial Services)

> "The only PQC platform built for banking — from cryptographic discovery through migration orchestration to continuous compliance. We help financial institutions protect their most sensitive data against the quantum threat while meeting evolving regulatory expectations from the OCC, Fed, SEC, and FINRA."

### Three Positioning Pillars

#### Pillar 1: Regulatory Readiness

"Be ready before the examiner asks."

- Pre-built compliance mapping to FFIEC IT Handbook, OCC Comptroller's Handbook, Fed SR letters
- Examination preparation documentation and dashboards
- Board-ready quantum risk reporting
- Alignment with G7 CEG roadmap phases

**Target buyer**: CCO and CISO jointly

#### Pillar 2: HNDL Risk Reduction

"Your encrypted data is being harvested today. Protect what matters most."

- Financial-services-specific HNDL risk quantification
- Data type prioritization (M&A > Trading > PII > SWIFT)
- Measurable risk reduction metrics
- Automated sensitivity classification

**Target buyer**: CRO and CISO jointly

#### Pillar 3: Crypto Agility

"Migrate once, adapt forever."

- Platform approach that survives algorithm changes
- Integration with existing HSM/PKI infrastructure
- No vendor lock-in to single crypto provider
- Future-proof architecture that handles NIST algorithm updates

**Target buyer**: CTO/CIO

### Messaging Don'ts

- **Don't lead with quantum computing** — most bank executives are skeptical of quantum hype; lead with *regulatory risk* and *data protection*
- **Don't claim certainty about Q-Day timing** — credibility requires acknowledging uncertainty; frame as risk management under uncertainty
- **Don't position as point solution** — banks are consolidating vendors, not adding new ones; position as platform or managed service
- **Don't use fear-mongering** — sophisticated bank buyers (CISOs, CROs) reject FUD; use data, regulatory trajectories, and peer benchmarks
