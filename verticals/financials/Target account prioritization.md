# Target Account Prioritization for PQC in Financial Services

## Strategic Intelligence Brief — March 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Not all financial institutions are equally ready for — or equally receptive to — PQC solutions. Prioritization must account for three variables: (1) **urgency** (regulatory pressure, HNDL exposure, data sensitivity), (2) **readiness** (existing crypto programs, innovation culture, budget availability), and (3) **accessibility** (can we reach decision-makers, is there an existing relationship, what's the procurement friction). The ideal initial targets are institutions where all three variables score high.

**Bottom line**: JPMorgan and HSBC are too far ahead to need a new vendor. The sweet spot is large banks that *know* they need to act but haven't yet committed to a vendor — BNY Mellon, Goldman Sachs, Wells Fargo, Citigroup, and select large regionals.

## Financial Market Infrastructure (FMI) Targets

FMIs are uniquely critical because they underpin the entire financial system. A quantum vulnerability in an FMI affects every bank connected to it.

| Institution | Role | HNDL Exposure | Priority |
|------------|------|---------------|----------|
| **DTCC** | Central securities depository; clearing | CRITICAL — processes $2.5Q+ annually | HIGH |
| **ICE/NYSE** | Exchange operator | CRITICAL — trading data, listing data | HIGH |
| **CME Group** | Derivatives clearing | CRITICAL — futures/options positions | HIGH |
| **CLS Group** | FX settlement | HIGH — FX settlement for 18 currencies | MODERATE-HIGH |
| **SWIFT** | Payment messaging | Already planning PQC roadmap — partner, not sell to | PARTNER |
| **Visa/Mastercard** | Payment networks | CRITICAL — card data, merchant data | HIGH (but own security teams are large) |

**FMI strategy**: These institutions amplify the sales story — "DTCC is moving to PQC. Are your connections to DTCC quantum-safe?" FMIs also have unique procurement dynamics (fewer vendors, longer evaluation, but once in, very sticky).

- **Bank of America** — Patent portfolio suggests proprietary approach; hard to sell to a bank that's building its own
- **BNP Paribas** — SandboxAQ investor/customer; unlikely to evaluate alternative vendors
- **HSBC** — Already piloting with multiple partners (QKD + PQC); late to enter, but potential channel for international expansion

## Early Adopter Characteristics

Based on research, financial institutions most likely to be early PQC adopters share these characteristics:

1. **Active quantum computing engagement** (investment, research partnerships, or internal programs)
2. **CISO who reports to CRO or CEO** (budget independence and risk-framing ability)
3. **Recent consent order or MRA** (heightened sensitivity to regulatory gaps)
4. **NIST NCCoE participation** (self-selected as quantum-aware)
5. **Public quantum risk research** (Citi GPS, HSBC blockchain trials — signals board awareness)
6. **Active hiring for crypto/PQC roles** (BNY Mellon — confirms budget allocation)
7. **Strong custodian/fiduciary role** (duty of care extends to client data protection)
